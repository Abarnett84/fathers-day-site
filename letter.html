FATHER'S DAY • JUNE 2026
Dear Dad,
From your kid
(the one who still calls when the WiFi dies)

I made you a whole website. That should tell you something. Most people just send a text or buy a tie. I went full dad-joke + photo evidence mode. You're welcome.

Thank you for being the kind of dad who shows up. Not just for the big stuff — though you were there for that too — but for the boring Tuesday nights when I needed someone to tell me the lawnmower sound was "probably fine" even when it clearly wasn't.

You taught me how to fix things, how to laugh at myself, and how to tell a joke so bad that people groan and secretly love it. That last one might be your greatest legacy.

A few things I'm still blaming you for (lovingly):
  • → My terrible sense of direction (you said "just head north")
  • → The way I now explain everything with hand gestures
  • → Thinking I can "just take a look" at any broken appliance and fix it
  • → My addiction to bad puns (this one's on you, old man)

But mostly I want to say thank you for the stuff that actually matters. For making our house feel safe. For showing up even when you were tired. For being the person I still call when something breaks — or when I just need to hear that everything's going to be okay, even if we both know you're just winging it too.

You're not perfect. None of us are. But you're mine, and that's better than perfect.

OFFICIAL DAD CERTIFICATIONS
Master of "I'll Just Take a Look"
Professional Dad Joke Practitioner
World's Okayest but Actually Best Dad
Love you, Dad.
Your favorite (and only) kid • 2026
P.S. There's also a photo vault and a goofy homepage. I went a little overboard. You'll survive.
